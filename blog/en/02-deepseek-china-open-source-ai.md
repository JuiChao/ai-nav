# DeepSeek Explained: The Rise of China's Most Powerful Open-Source AI

In the global AI race, a company from Hangzhou, China is rising at a remarkable pace—DeepSeek. Through its open-source strategy and exceptional model performance, DeepSeek has become one of the most influential forces in the global open-source AI community.

## 1. Who Is DeepSeek?

DeepSeek was founded in 2023 by Liang Wenfeng, the founder of quantitative hedge fund High-Flyer. Unlike most AI startups, DeepSeek long refused external funding, operating independently with High-Flyer's own capital. This unique model allowed the team to focus on R&D rather than being driven by investor demands.

However, as of May 2026, reports indicate DeepSeek is considering its first large-scale external funding round, seeking up to **50 billion RMB** (approximately $7.35 billion), with an expected post-money valuation between **$45 billion and $51.5 billion**. China's National IC Industry Investment Fund ("Big Fund") is reportedly among the lead investors (Source: [Sina Finance](https://finance.sina.com.cn), [36Kr](https://36kr.com)).

## 2. Model Evolution: From V1 to V4

DeepSeek's iteration pace has been remarkably fast:

### DeepSeek-V3 (Early 2025)
The release that put DeepSeek on the global map. V3 matched or exceeded GPT-4 performance across multiple benchmarks while costing only a fraction of OpenAI's inference prices. The result sent shockwaves through Wall Street, causing volatility in chipmaker stocks like NVIDIA.

### DeepSeek-V4 Series (April 2026)
The latest flagship release includes two variants (Source: [Caixin Global](https://www.caixinglobal.com)):

| Model | Total Parameters | Active Parameters | Core Use Case |
|-------|-----------------|-------------------|---------------|
| **V4 Pro** | 1.6 trillion | 49 billion | Complex reasoning, tool calling |
| **V4 Flash** | 284 billion | 13 billion | Cost-efficient API services |

The V4 series employs a hybrid attention architecture, supports context windows of up to **1 million tokens**, and has been optimized for domestic Chinese chips such as Huawei's Ascend series (Source: [Shanghai Observer](https://www.shobserver.com)).

## 3. Open Source: DeepSeek's Core Advantage

DeepSeek's most notable strategy is its commitment to **full open source**. Model weights are published on Hugging Face and GitHub, freely available for anyone to download, use, and modify.

This strategy has had an enormous impact:

- **Global developer community**: DeepSeek models consistently rank among the top on developer platforms like OpenRouter
- **Industry ecosystem**: Countless Chinese companies build AI applications on DeepSeek models, lowering barriers to entry
- **Academic influence**: DeepSeek's technical papers are widely cited, and its MoE architecture has had a profound impact on the field

The **V2.5** series released in April 2026 uses the MIT open-source license, meaning virtually no restrictions even for commercial use (Source: [OSChina](https://www.oschina.net)).

## 4. Why DeepSeek Matters

### Breaking the "Compute Myth"
DeepSeek proved that world-class models can be trained with limited compute through algorithmic innovation and architectural optimization—strategically significant for China's AI industry amid chip export controls.

### Democratizing AI
The open-source strategy enables small businesses and independent developers to access top-tier AI models without relying on expensive commercial APIs.

### Reshaping Global Competition
DeepSeek's success proves that China can compete at the world-class level not just in AI applications, but in foundational models as well.

DeepSeek's rise is one of the most compelling stories in AI during 2025–2026. From technical prowess to open-source philosophy, from cost efficiency to ecosystem impact, DeepSeek is redefining what "Chinese AI" means on the world stage.
